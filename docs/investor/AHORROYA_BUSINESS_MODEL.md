# AhorroYA Business Model

Date: 2026-05-11

## Overview

AhorroYA can monetize through a layered model: consumer Premium, affiliate/commerce referrals, ads, B2B data insights, and retail partnerships. The correct order is to validate user value and retention before scaling monetization.

## Revenue Streams

### 1. Premium

Potential benefits:

- Price-drop alerts.
- Favorites and alerts limits lifted.
- Price history.
- Smart cart optimization.
- Comparison by zone.
- Monthly savings summary.
- Fewer/no ads if ads are introduced.

Current state:

- Premium UI exists.
- PayPal sandbox subscription flow exists.
- Live monetization is blocked by PayPal live credentials and tests.

### 2. Affiliates

Potential:

- Product/catalog outbound clicks.
- Commerce partner links.
- Commission per click, order, or lead.

Current state:

- Product link and commerce click tracking exists.
- Affiliate commercial agreements are not yet validated.

### 3. Ads

Potential:

- Sponsored savings placements.
- Local store promotions.
- CPG brand campaigns.

Risks:

- Ads can reduce trust if mixed with "cheapest" recommendations.
- Sponsored content must be labeled.

### 4. B2B Data Insights

Potential customers:

- Brands.
- Retailers.
- Local distributors.
- Market researchers.

Possible products:

- Search demand reports.
- Price spread reports.
- Category movement.
- Regional demand signals.

Current state:

- Some B2B/protected endpoints exist.
- Product is not mature enough to sell as production-grade insights yet.

### 5. Partnerships With Commerce

Potential:

- Local stores provide price feeds.
- AhorroYA routes demand to partner stores.
- Premium users get alerts or offers.

Current state:

- Requires sales development and data partnership validation.

## Uruguay First

Why Uruguay first:

- Smaller market enables focused validation.
- Montevideo has dense retail behavior.
- WhatsApp is a natural acquisition channel.
- Grocery savings is a clear consumer pain.

## LATAM Later

Expansion criteria:

- Reproducible data ingestion process.
- Proof of retention in Uruguay.
- Partner or official price sources.
- Localized store/catalog adapters.
- Country-by-country payment and compliance review.

## Preliminary Unit Economics

These are assumptions, not actual traction:

| Metric | Conservative assumption | Base assumption | Aggressive assumption |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Free-to-Premium conversion | 1% | 3% | 6% |
| Monthly Premium ARPU | USD 2-4 | USD 4-6 | USD 6-8 |
| Monthly churn | 10-15% | 6-10% | 3-6% |
| CAC first 100 users | Near zero/manual | Low/community | Paid experiments |
| Gross margin software | High | High | High |

## Key Assumptions

- Users care enough about grocery savings to repeat searches.
- Price data is sufficiently fresh and trusted.
- WhatsApp sharing produces organic acquisition.
- Premium value is clear after the first savings moment.
- Live PayPal and OAuth gates can be completed without major rework.

## What Must Be Proven

1. Search-to-result activation.
2. Repeat usage.
3. Share rate.
4. Favorite/alert retention.
5. Premium willingness to pay.
6. Reliable data coverage.
7. Commercial partner interest.
